Why Choose Piano?

The piano is more than just an instrument—it's a gateway to mastering music and enhancing life skills.

  • The piano is a child-friendly instrument with a simple black-and-white key arrangement that displays all the notes.
  • Pianos are commonly used for music composition due to their ability to play both chords and melodies simultaneously.
  • Playing the piano increases your academic achievement in all subjects, particularly science and math. It enhances brain activity on both sides, potentially increasing IQ by a few points over time.
  • Playing the piano with both melodies and chords at the same time increases your hand strength and ambidexterity (the ability to use both the right and left hand equally well).
  • Knowing how to play the piano can help you learn other musical instruments. Piano playing fosters a good understanding of music theory, rhythm, and hand-eye coordination, which can be applied to other instruments.
  • Many people who perform music believe it brings them to a peaceful state, thereby reducing stress and anxiety.
  • Playing the piano also improves your aural skills, confidence, and general enjoyment.

FAQs

- Do I need 88 keys to learn piano?

A beginner may learn to play with 66 keys, while most music can be played on a 72-key instrument. However, if you want to play classical piano and take grade exams, you should have all 88 keys.
